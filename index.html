The Case for YesHello

In our fast-paced, efficiency-driven world, the "nohello" movement has gained traction by advocating for skipping simple greetings in online communication. The goal is to respect people's time and increase clarity by diving straight into the main point. While this approach has its merits, I believe it also reflects a troubling aspect of modern life: the relentless rush and prioritization of efficiency over human connection.

I propose an alternative: the YesHello Movement. This movement aims to balance clarity and efficiency with kindness and humanity, emphasizing that being nice and friendly should never be an option but a standard practice.

Implementing YesHello: Practical Tips

  • Brief, Purposeful Greetings: Begin your messages with a short greeting that acknowledges the recipient before moving on to the main point. Example: "Hello [Name], I hope you're well. I wanted to discuss [specific topic]."
  • Clarity with Warmth: Combine clarity with a touch of warmth in your messages. Example: "Hi [Name], just a quick note to ask about [specific question]. Looking forward to your response. Thanks!"
  • Respect Time and Context: Be mindful of the context and the recipient's time, but don't forgo basic politeness. Example: "Good morning [Name], I have a quick question about [topic]. Could you please assist me with this when you have a moment?"

Join the YesHello Movement

The YesHello Movement is about striking a balance between efficiency and humanity in our communications. It encourages us to be clear and respectful of time while also fostering kindness and friendliness. By adopting this approach, we can create a more positive, connected, and humane digital communication environment.
